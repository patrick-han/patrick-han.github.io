TAing for the course that inspired my interest in embedded development.

Scope of the course
======
ELEC 327 is a junior spring lab course that is more or less required (there is a limited selection of alterantive lab courses). The course is essentially an introduction to embedded systems programming using the MSP430G2553. Assignments consist of a series of labs/small projects in which students learn how to implement a new concept in the form of a small device. Topics include: Timers, PWM, Low Power Modes, PCB Design, ADCs, Serial Communications, and Software Design.


Responsibilities
======
My responsbility as a TA was to hold help sessions 2-3 times a week to help students debug their devices in the lab. Sometimes that involved using lab equipment to debug boards, and other times it was a software issue.

Outcomes + Lessons Learned
======
Embedded design really does require a lot of reading (of datasheets). In general I try to encourage people to consult the datasheet first if they have a question. Not because I don't want to give them an answer, but sometimes I don't remember myself!
