My first TA experience.

Scope of the course
======
During the fall semester of my senior year I TA'd for the junior level class ELEC 301: Signals, Systems and Machine learning.
The first half of the class essentially serves as a more complete picture of DSP, since we spend most of sophomore year discussing signal processing
in the realm of continious time. 

The second half of the class moves quickly as an introduction to machine learning. We start with linear regression
and cover most of the common algorithms and techniques: KNN, K-means, SVMs, etc. The course ends with an introduction to neural networks. An emphasis
was placed on a strong grasp of linear algebra with some emphasis in elementary probability and statistics (a class that is usaully taken concurrently).


Responsibilities
======
As an undergraduate teaching assistant I had two primary roles:

1. Facilitate homework help sessions once or twice a week

2. Create and deploy a Kaggle competition (Final Project for the class)

Homework help sessions were fairly straightforward, and I generally try to push students to reference specific concepts in the slides or from their
previous classes rather than directly giving them the method or solution.

For the final project, we chose to do a small subset of the [Freesound audio tagging challenge](https://www.kaggle.com/c/freesound-audio-tagging) (subset since computing resources were relatively limited). In total there was a team of around 6 TAs and I essentially took a lead in coordinating efforts between different subteams: Data-prep, handout writeup and Kaggle page creation.

Outcomes + Lessons Learned
======
Homework help sessions are usually fairly easy and straightforward. I think having to coordinate the creation of the Kaggle competition taught me a lot about being prepared to answer all sorts of questions. In initial writeups of the handout I thought we had most of the edge cases nailed, but someone always manages to come up with a creative inquiry.
