I've been listening to this podcast called [Triple Click](https://maximumfun.org/podcasts/triple-click/) recently, it's a games podcast that has a format I enjoy, and the length is appropriate enough. On the first episode, the main topic is that of: "What Video Games Assume You Know". It's an interesting topic that has definitely been covered before, but I just wanted to make a list of video game traits and mechanics that fall under "things you should know". Obviously, not every single one of these is a universal truth for all games across all genres, but it's interesting to note how common these traits actually are.

**"General Knowledge" in Platformers:**
1. In sidescrolling platformers, you move from left to right to get to the end of the level.
2. If you drop down off the platforms it's game over.
3. You can't just walk up to a creature, touching them will "hurt" your character in some way
4. In 3D platformers, use one joystick to move and the other to adjust your camera. In particular, the left and right ones, respectively.
5. Double tap or hold the jump button to jump higher.

**"General Knowledge" in (First Person Shooter) FPS games:**
1. If using a keyboard, WASD to move, left click to shoot, right click to aim-down-sights (ADS), SHIFT to sprint, CTRL to crouch/prone, reload with R.
2. If using a controller left joystick to move, right joystick to aim, left trigger to ADS, right trigger to shoot, click on the left joystick to sprint, one of the buttons (Usually "B" on an Xbox or "Circle" on a Playstation) to crouch/prone, reload with "X" on an Xbox or "Square" on a Playstation.

**"General Knowledge" in RPGs:**
1. You improve your character by "leveling up" and "and gaining experience".
2. You gain experience by performing quests or slaying enemies.
3. Sometimes, you can have a party of characters rather than just one, and each one has to be leveled separately.
4. There are different classes of characters: Warrior, Magic, Long-Range, etc. The names used in games are too varied to list.
