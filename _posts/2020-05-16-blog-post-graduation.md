The past few weeks have been pretty weird, having to study and do classes online. I'm graduating tomorrow, and as cringe as it is to say this, these past 4 years have definitely flown by and I'll really miss the time I spent at Rice. Honestly, I'm personally okay with not having a physical commencement, although I realize for many families (especially those who are first generation students), it would be devestating to not have one. For them, I sincerely hope the university arranges something as soon as possible. 

For me, however, I miss my friends the most, and the spontaneity of the time we spent together each and every day. I mean, this is basically the only time in your life where you can wake up and see your friends 24/7. In Rice's case, the campus and student population are so small, and it's really difficult to not see someone you know every hour, for better or worse. The coronavirus suddenly cutting everything short makes the end to undergrad bittersweet.

So if any underclassman or seniors read this, do yourself a favor and try to cherish each moment.
